Notes

The methodologies used in these spreadsheets is at an early stage of development. The spreadsheets are known to stop or become mixed up. All results should be double-checked.

The charts are built using Adobe Flash and thus will not display on certain smartphones or tablets.

If a chart fails to display, do try to open the spreadsheet. The charts update every five minutes, while the spreadsheets update every minute or so.

Once a chart opens, click on 'Edit this page' in order to access the full spreadsheet and its tabs. If you want to edit things, click on File menu > Make a copy...

Each spreadsheet has two visualization worksheets - see the tabs at the bottom of each spreadsheet.

The sheet titled 'Gadget1' needs a refresh to update. Refresh by pressing F5 or View Menu > Reload or Control-R. Gadget1 is better when you are on an old computer or have a slow Internet connection. Also you can save certain default settings such as starting up with a log scale.

Chart1 updates automatically every minute or so. Use Chart1 if you have a computer with a good graphics card and a fast Internet connection.

Chart parameters:

  • X-Axis shows the percent of gain or loss of a stock since yesterdays close. Look for the zero somewhere near the middle
  • Y-Axis shows the percent of the current volume compared to the average daily volume. All stock start a zero in the morning and move up during the course of the day.
  • The diameter of a dot is its relative market capitalization. The larger the market capitalization, the bigger the dot.
  • The color of the symbol indicates the business sector of the company.

Suggestions:

  • Move your mouse over any of the dots to display its symbol letter and associated data.
  • Make a zooming window by clicking twice in the chart area. Then click on "Zoom In".
  • When zoomed in, pan around the display by moving the box in Zoom window in lower right.
  • Hold your mouse over the colored boxes in the Sector labels area to flash the stocks in a sector.
  • Choose what data to display by clicking on the titles to each Axis
